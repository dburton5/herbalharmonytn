Most people think of watermelon as a pool/party snack. But when you cold-press it into juice, you’re extracting one of nature’s most underrated performance and recovery drinks and it fits perfectly into a fasting lifestyle.

The Star Compound: L-Citrulline

Watermelon is one of the only foods naturally dense in L-citrulline, an amino acid your kidneys convert into L-arginine, which then triggers nitric oxide (NO) production. Nitric oxide dilates your blood vessels improving circulation, lowering blood pressure, and flooding your muscles with oxygen. That’s the same pathway that pre-workout supplements try to activate synthetically.

The kicker? The rind has even more L-citrulline than the flesh. Cold-pressing the whole fruit gives you the full spectrum.

What Watermelon Juice Supports

	•	Cardiovascular health — Studies show regular consumption can reduce systolic blood pressure

	•	Muscle recovery — L-citrulline reduces delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS) post-lift

	•	Deep hydration — Watermelon is 92% water, packed with potassium and magnesium for intracellular hydration ideal after sauna or fasting

	•	Anti-inflammatory defense — Lycopene (higher bioavailability than tomatoes) and Vitamin C combat oxidative stress

	•	Kidney support — Gentle diuretic action flushes the urinary tract without depleting electrolytes




How I Use It

My favorite way to stack it: beet juice + watermelon juice as a post-fast break or pre-workout drink. Beet juice activates the same nitric oxide pathway from the other direction you get compounded circulation support that’s hard to beat naturally.

Simple recipe:

	•	2 cups watermelon 

	•	½ cup beet juice (cold-pressed)

	•	Squeeze of lime 

	•	Pinch of Himalayan salt

	•	Optional: fresh mint or ginger

Cold-press or blend, drink fresh best consumed within 24 - 48 hours




A Note on Timing

Watermelon juice is one of the best fasting break options gentle on a fasted stomach, easy to digest, hydrating immediately. If you’re doing 16:8, this is a clean, intentional way to open your eating window.

Who Should Use Caution

If you’re on blood pressure medication or have chronic kidney disease, talk to your healthcare provider first. The potassium content and vasodilatory effects are real. Those with IBS or fructose sensitivity may also want to start with smaller amounts.
