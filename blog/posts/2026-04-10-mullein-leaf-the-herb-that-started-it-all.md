## What Is Mullein?

Mullein (*Verbascum thapsus*) is a woolly-leafed biennial plant you'll find growing wild across the United States and Europe. Most people walk right past it, it looks like a weed. During its first year, it forms a low rosette of large, fuzzy leaves. In its second year, it shoots up a tall stem with yellow flowers.

Here's what caught my attention: this plant has been used medicinally since at least 450 BCE. It has been identified as a powerful anti-inflammatory agent since more than over two thousand years ago. It goes by other names too: candlewick, lungwort, velvet plant.

## Why Mullein Works for Respiratory Health

Mullein isn't a one-trick herb. There are multiple compounds working together to support your lungs.

**Saponins** are the heavy hitters they help loosen mucus so your body can actually clear out congestion. If you've ever had that deep, stubborn chest tightness, this is the compound doing the work.

**Mucilage** is what makes mullein so soothing. It coats irritated tissues in your respiratory tract, calming inflammation and reducing that raw, scratchy feeling in your throat and chest.

**Flavonoids** like hesperidin and verbascoside bring anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They're working at the cellular level to reduce swelling and protect your tissues.

On top of that, mullein contains **phenylethanol glycosides** that give it antimicrobial properties  meaning it may help your body fight off respiratory infections, not just manage symptoms.

## What Mullein Can Do For You

The biggest benefit is respiratory support. Traditionally, mullein has been used for coughs, bronchitis, and asthma-related symptoms. It acts as an expectorant helping you clear congestion while simultaneously soothing the irritated tissues that make you want to cough in the first place.

Beyond the lungs, mullein has shown antimicrobial activity against certain bacteria and viruses. There's traditional use for urinary tract support and even topical wound healing, though the research there is still limited.

## How I Use It

The simplest way to start is **mullein tea**. Take 1-2 teaspoons of dried mullein leaf, steep it in hot water for 10-15 minutes, and drink up to three times daily. One important tip: strain it well through a fine mesh or cheesecloth. The tiny hairs on the leaves can irritate your throat if they get through.

Other options include tinctures (2-4 ml, three times daily) and capsules (typically 300-500mg, following the product directions). Mullein also works well combined with other respiratory herbs.

I'll say this when I started drinking mullein tea consistently, the difference in my breathing was noticeable within days. Not weeks, not months. Days.

## A Few Things to Keep in Mind

Mullein is generally well-tolerated, but there are a few things worth knowing. Safety during pregnancy hasn't been established, so consult your healthcare provider if that applies. It may interact with diabetes medications and diuretics. There are some historical traditions of smoking mullein, keep in mind, it can irritate your lungs, which can feel like it defeats the purpose.

Also, quality matters. Not all mullein products are created equal. Look for reputable sources that prioritize purity.
